Play Suntower View source code

Suntower is a rage platformer with a focus on rope physics. Made for GWJ #66 with the theme "Escape," the game is about a sunflower trying to escape from the bottom of a decrepit tower. As a player, you navigate between beams of sunlight to grow the sunflower and use your head to latch onto higher platforms, pulling yourself up. I received tons of positive feedback from playtesting by dozens of my friends, with reactions ranging between unbridled rage at falling over and over in this ONE SPOT, to love for the charming aesthetic and upbeat soundtrack.

  • Created a linked rope data structure with accurate rope physics, allowing for both swinging and extension/retraction
  • Produced pixel art assets independently, alternating between art and programming in an iterative process
  • Made a day-night sunlight cycle with light beams that physically collide with the player through raycasting
  • Designed levels and obstacles with intentional care for which heights and shapes are accessible and inaccessible to the player

Play Nullify Light Source code available upon request

Nullify Light is a bullet hell where you absorb and reuse bullets against an angel trying to purge you from the world. I organized a group of friends to ideate and discuss mechanics for the game, resulting in a fresh take on bullet hells where you can actually delete bullets. This core mechanic opens the door for typically undodgable or impossible patterns to become reasonable for a skilled player.

  • Created efficient bullet spawning systems, making use of Godot built-ins like signals and Tweens
  • Produced art assets with precise bullet hell hitboxes in mind
  • Implemented object pooling for bullets to avoid performance issues despite hundreds of bullets on-screen
  • Independently coded and also pair-programmed with a partner in consistent meetings to make steady progress
  • Led group game design discussions, nailing down core mechanics while maintaining a reasonable scope for a game jam

Made for a Japanese class final project, Kanjou Meisou (感情瞑想, meaning "emotional meditation") is a meditation simulator about getting in touch with your feelings (and less in touch with TikTok). I made this game as part of a presentation on solutions to anxiety, and so I was happy to hear from a friend that this game helped them manage their feelings during a hard time.

  • Implemented a mouse movement tracking system as a form of measuring consistency and focus in the meditation simulation
  • Primarily focused on visuals and aesthetic in gameplay, adding polished animations and smoothing to various elements
  • Utilized different sound effects to simulate the phone usage and meditation experiences
  • Planned the game out with a tight scope, to be finished efficiently during finals period

Final Farm is a spin on the typical tower defense, giving the player an active role in maintaining and assisting their towers during combat. Made in 9 days, yet definitely the most mechanically complex game on this list, Final Farm is a high-priority project in my free time.

  • Ideated and implemented a customizable targeting system for both towers and enemies, optimized for large numbers
  • Drew animations and coded specific behaviors for many different plants and insects
  • Made a fully functioning upgrade system for plants, allowing for different scaling per value per plant
  • Implemented a smooth shop menu showing each plant's stats in a readable format, allowing for upgrade previews
  • Added an editor-exposed wave customization system allowing for full flexibility in when and how enemies spawn in a wave
  • Calculated a fine-tunable difficulty curve for enemy wave difficulties based on game progress
